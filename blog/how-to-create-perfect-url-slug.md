## Introduction: Why Getting Your Slug Right Matters

A URL slug is the part of a web address that comes after the domain name and identifies a specific page in a human-readable format. For example, in `https://example.com/how-to-bake-sourdough-bread`, the slug is `how-to-bake-sourdough-bread`.

Getting your URL slug right is not just a cosmetic detail. It has real consequences for your website's performance:

- **SEO rankings**: Search engines use the slug to understand what a page is about. A clean, keyword-rich slug gives you a ranking advantage over a messy, auto-generated one.
- **Click-through rates**: Users see the URL in search results. A readable slug like `/best-running-shoes-2024` inspires more confidence and clicks than `/p?id=48291&ref=cat3`.
- **Shareability**: When people share your link on social media, in emails, or in messaging apps, a clean URL looks professional and trustworthy.
- **Longevity**: Once a URL is published and indexed, changing it requires redirects and risks breaking inbound links. Getting it right the first time saves you headaches down the road.

The good news is that creating a perfect slug is straightforward. Follow the seven steps below, and you will produce clean, optimized URLs every single time.

## Step 1: Start With Your Page Title or Target Keyword

Every good slug begins with a meaningful starting point. That is usually your page title or the primary keyword you want the page to rank for.

For example, if your blog post is titled:

> "10 Tips for Writing Better JavaScript Code in 2024!"

Then that full title is your raw material. Write it down as-is before making any changes.

### Why the target keyword matters

If your page is optimized for a specific search term, consider using that keyword phrase as your starting point instead of the full title. A slug built around `writing-better-javascript` is often more effective than one that mirrors a long, conversational title word for word.

## Step 2: Remove Stop Words

Stop words are common words that add grammatical structure but carry little meaning for search engines or readers scanning a URL. Examples include:

- a, an, the
- is, are, was, were
- in, on, at, to, for
- of, and, or, but
- it, this, that

Taking our example title, "10 Tips for Writing Better JavaScript Code in 2024!", removing stop words gives us:

`10 Tips Writing Better JavaScript Code 2024`

Be careful not to remove words that change the meaning. In a slug like `how-to-tie-a-tie`, removing "a" is fine, but removing "how" and "to" would strip the instructional intent. Use your judgment.

## Step 3: Convert to Lowercase

URLs are case-sensitive on most web servers. A request to `/My-Page` and `/my-page` can return different results, leading to duplicate content issues and confused users. The universal convention is to use all lowercase letters in your slugs.

Applying this to our example:

`10 tips writing better javascript code 2024`

This is a simple rule with no exceptions. Always lowercase.

## Step 4: Replace Spaces With Hyphens

Spaces are not valid characters in URLs. Browsers encode them as `%20`, which produces ugly, unreadable addresses. The standard practice is to replace every space with a hyphen (`-`).

Our example becomes:

`10-tips-writing-better-javascript-code-2024`

### Hyphens vs. underscores

Google treats hyphens as word separators but treats underscores as word joiners. The slug `writing-better-javascript` is read as three separate words, while `writing_better_javascript` may be read as one compound term. Always use hyphens.

## Step 5: Remove Special Characters and Accents

Slugs should only contain lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens. Strip out everything else:

- Punctuation: `!`, `?`, `.`, `,`, `:`, `;`, `'`, `"`
- Symbols: `@`, `#`, `$`, `%`, `&`, `*`, `(`, `)`
- Accented characters: replace them with their ASCII equivalents (e.g., `e` instead of `e`)

Our example title originally had an exclamation mark. After step 2, that was already removed along with the stop words, so our slug remains:

`10-tips-writing-better-javascript-code-2024`

If we had started with a title like "Beginner's Guide to Cafe Culture," the slug would become `beginners-guide-cafe-culture` -- the apostrophe is dropped and the accent on "cafe" is normalized.

## Step 6: Keep It Under 60 Characters

Shorter slugs are easier to read, easier to remember, and less likely to get truncated in search results or when shared. Aim for a slug that is under 60 characters. If yours is longer, trim less essential words from the end or middle.

Our current slug is 47 characters, well within the limit:

`10-tips-writing-better-javascript-code-2024` (47 characters)

If we had a longer one like `the-complete-beginners-guide-to-writing-better-javascript-code-in-2024-and-beyond`, we would trim it down to something like `beginners-guide-writing-better-javascript-2024`.

### Quick length guidelines

- **Ideal**: 3 to 5 words (20-40 characters)
- **Acceptable**: up to 8 words (under 60 characters)
- **Too long**: anything above 60 characters -- trim it

## Step 7: Review for Readability

Before you publish, read the slug out loud. Ask yourself:

- Does it clearly describe the page content?
- Would a user understand what the page is about just by reading the URL?
- Is it free of awkward abbreviations or confusing word combinations?

Our final slug passes all three checks:

`10-tips-writing-better-javascript-code-2024`

It is descriptive, scannable, and professional.

## Full Example Walkthrough

Let us walk through another example from start to finish. Suppose your article title is:

> "The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Best Running Shoes for Beginners (2024 Edition!)"

| Step | Action | Result |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Start with the title | The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Best Running Shoes for Beginners (2024 Edition!) |
| 2 | Remove stop words | Ultimate Guide Choosing Best Running Shoes Beginners 2024 Edition |
| 3 | Convert to lowercase | ultimate guide choosing best running shoes beginners 2024 edition |
| 4 | Replace spaces with hyphens | `ultimate-guide-choosing-best-running-shoes-beginners-2024-edition` |
| 5 | Remove special characters | `ultimate-guide-choosing-best-running-shoes-beginners-2024-edition` |
| 6 | Trim to under 60 characters | `best-running-shoes-beginners-2024` |
| 7 | Review for readability | Clear, concise, keyword-rich. Ready to publish. |

The final slug is:

`https://example.com/best-running-shoes-beginners-2024`

Clean, short, and optimized for both search engines and human readers.
